Records verification

Document status must be clear before publication.

The archive uses availability labels so public readers can distinguish public summaries from redacted, controlled, private, or withheld materials.

Availability labels

Each label communicates a publication boundary.

Public summary

Summary suitable for public review

High-level description of what a record category shows, why it matters, and what question it supports.

Redacted

Limited excerpt or document

Material may be published only after removing private addresses, third-party identities, health details, and sensitive identifiers.

Controlled

Formal review only

Material may be shared with authorized reviewers, oversight bodies, counsel, or agencies, but not published broadly.

Private

Withheld from public archive

Medical details, participant-level records, private evidence, and sensitive correspondence remain withheld unless a lawful and appropriate disclosure basis exists.

Verification Checklist

Records should pass this review before upload.

  • Confirm the record category and date range.
  • Separate the factual record from participant-prepared analysis.
  • Remove private addresses, health details, and third-party identifying information.
  • Label controlled materials as controlled rather than public.
  • Use summaries where publication of raw evidence would create privacy risk.
