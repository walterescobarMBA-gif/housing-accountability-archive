Confidentiality

Privacy protection is part of the archive structure.

The archive is designed to support public-interest review while avoiding exposure of sensitive participant-level records, medical details, private addresses, third-party identities, and controlled evidence.

Do not publish

Raw medical records, diagnosis documents, private addresses, third-party participant names, private account identifiers, and sensitive evidence are not public archive materials.

Use summaries first

When a record supports a public-interest question, summarize the category, date range, and issue without exposing unnecessary private detail.

Control distribution

Materials needed for formal review can be kept outside the public site and shared through appropriate controlled channels.

Publication rule:

Public transparency does not require publishing every underlying record. The archive favors verification, redaction, summary, and controlled availability when raw publication would create privacy risk.
