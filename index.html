Housing Accountability Archive

Documenting Housing. Advancing Accountability.

Housing Accountability Archive is an independent public-interest documentation project focused on publicly funded housing program administration, records verification, oversight transparency, and participant-centered accountability.

HAA reviews policies, public records, monitoring materials, administrative correspondence, and participant-level documentation to evaluate whether required housing-program actions can be verified through records.

Guiding Question

When governing authorities require documentation, can the required administrative record actually be produced?

About HAA

A records-based accountability publication.

Housing Accountability Archive was created to examine whether publicly funded housing programs can verify administrative compliance through records. The project focuses on documentation, oversight transparency, participant-facing procedures, and administrative systems that affect housing stability.

Mission

HAA documents housing-program administration, identifies records-verification gaps, and supports public accountability through structured review of policies, monitoring records, correspondence, and participant-level documentation.

Review Principle

HAA does not begin with assumptions about legal fault or misconduct. Its review model asks whether required actions, safeguards, services, communications, and oversight steps can be verified through documentary evidence.

HAA asks what documentation should exist, who should maintain it, whether it was produced, and what remains unresolved.

What HAA Is

  • An independent documentation project.
  • A public-interest archive.
  • A records-based review model.
  • A participant-centered accountability publication.

What HAA Is Not

  • HAA is not a government agency.
  • HAA is not a law firm.
  • HAA is not a legal-services provider.
  • HAA is not an official investigative authority.
Current Review

HAA-2026-001

Publicly Funded Housing Program Administration

A Records-Based Review of Administrative Documentation, Program Oversight, and Participant-Level Verification

Publication Series Annual Compliance Review
Version 2.0 | Authority Stack Review
Review Period July 2025 through July 2026
Status Records Verification Draft

HAA-2026-001 evaluates whether participant-facing housing program administration can be independently verified through documentary evidence.

The review examines administrative responsibility, provider policies, participant-file documentation, housing-plan records, supportive-service records, monitoring reports, grievance routing, records retention, and administrative continuity.

Publication Notice: No full evidence packet or sensitive participant-level file is published during the verification stage. Findings, exhibits, entity responses, nonresponses, and records-access updates may be incorporated into the final review as appropriate.
Methodology

Authority Stack Review Model

HAA applies an authority-stack methodology comparing governing standards against actual records. The purpose is to identify what documentation should exist, who should maintain it, whether it was produced, and what remains unresolved.

  1. Federal Authority Federal statutes, regulations, program requirements, civil-rights standards, confidentiality obligations, and administrative-control requirements.
  2. Local Administrative Authority Local housing department policies, procedures, monitoring standards, reporting requirements, and oversight expectations.
  3. Provider Authority Provider policies, procedures, participant-facing forms, grievance procedures, service standards, and subcontractor oversight requirements.
  4. Monitoring Records Official monitoring reports, sampled-file findings, corrective-action records, site-visit records, and administrative follow-up.
  5. Participant Documentary Record Participant-level records, correspondence, notices, requests, responses, file-access history, and entity communications.
Review Domains

Compliance domains under review.

Each domain is reviewed using the same structure: applicable authorities, administrative standard, required documentation, evidence reviewed, observations, verification status, recommendations, and evidence IDs.

Governance

Administrative responsibility, oversight roles, sponsor accountability, and subcontractor coordination.

Participant Files

Eligibility, assessments, housing plans, case notes, referrals, and required client-file documentation.

Supportive Services

Service planning, referrals, housing-search support, follow-up, transition planning, and documentation.

Housing Quality

Site visits, inspections, room-entry controls, habitability documentation, and privacy safeguards.

Grievance Routing

Intake, jurisdiction, routing, response windows, determination records, and participant-facing instructions.

Records Access

Records availability, retention, custody, public-records production, and file-access procedures.

Records Verification

Verification before publication.

HAA uses a records-based verification process before finalizing major reviews. The purpose is not to litigate facts in public. The purpose is to determine whether the administrative record can substantiate required actions, services, safeguards, and oversight steps.

Verification Questions

  • What authority applies?
  • What administrative action was required?
  • What records should exist?
  • Who should maintain those records?
  • Were the records produced or identified?
  • Are records missing, incomplete, disputed, or pending?
  • What verification status applies?

Entity Responses

Entities receiving a records-verification notice may respond only as to records within their custody, control, routing history, or program responsibility.

Responses may state that records exist, do not exist, are partially available, are held by another custodian, were transferred, were destroyed under a retention policy, are pending review, are disputed, or require clarification.

Verification Ratings

Rating Meaning
Verified Required documentation was produced or directly confirmed.
Substantially Verified Core documentation exists, with minor gaps.
Partially Verified Some records exist, but key elements remain missing.
Indeterminate Available records are insufficient to verify either way.
Not Verified Required records were requested or expected but not produced, identified, or confirmed.
Important Note: Records-verification requests are not intended to replace public-records requests, grievances, appeals, legal proceedings, or formal oversight investigations. They provide a structured opportunity to clarify the administrative record before publication or further escalation.
Confidentiality

Sensitive information is handled with restraint.

HAA’s purpose is accountability through documentation, not unnecessary disclosure of private participant information.

Sensitive Information

HAA limits disclosure of:

  • medical information;
  • financial records;
  • identity documents;
  • personal-property details;
  • private correspondence;
  • participant-file materials;
  • sensitive housing-location details;
  • personal contact information;
  • records involving third parties.

Verification Stage

During records-verification stages, HAA may limit communications to issue categories, record descriptions, verification questions, authority references, custody questions, and status summaries.

HAA does not transmit a full evidence packet when doing so would unnecessarily disclose sensitive information.

Public accountability does not require careless exposure of private records.
